BikeLink

BikeLINK is a cross-platform app that allows users to find the best bike routes in the city.

The Project

The Problem

The people are not being able to find the proper infrastructure to ride their bikes around the city and are looking for a digital service solution

My Role

Approach

Users

Users interviewed

  • People who think about using a bike as their main mode for transportation, but are not fully convinced yet
  • Biking beginners who need assistance and want to get to know the city better
  • Biking experts who are mainly looking for a powerful navigation tool

    • Questions asked

  • How often do you use your bike and for which purpose?
  • Do you wear a helmet?
  • Do you use your bike for long distances?
  • Do you know your city well?
  • Do you use any apps to help you find route? If yes, which ones?
  • What do you like in the app you use and what don't you like?
  • Could you tell me about the time you had any difficulty while cycling, or even an accident (if it ever happened)?

    • Jobs to be done

    When I want to So that
    I cycle to a new destination Find the most appealing route I can cycle without stress
    I use BikeLink Be able to save routes I will be able to set direction quickly
    I select a certain route Be informed about the road conditions I can cycle safely
    The route has some road conditions Be able to select another route I won't waste time looking for the new route when I am already cycling
    I plan to cycle Be alarmed or notified about weather conditions Be precautious and prepared
    I cycle Be able report the road conditions Contribute to the information base and help other cyclists

    Solution

    For the card-sorting I prepared 3 categories which were supposed to contain certain features:
    • Homepage
    • Saved Routes
    • Route Choosing
    The users’ task was to intuitively sort the offered cards to see how well they would understand the app’s structure.
